About

In the spring of 2014 I completed my Doctorate in Communications and Media Studies from the University of Illinois. During my PhD I taught and researched topics in new media, technology studies, and cultural studies. I also spent a lot of time knitting.

All the knitting I did made me realize how much I like making things. When I finished my PhD I enrolled inGeneral Assembly's Web Development Immersive bootcampto learn how to make things that didn't involve yarn or needles.

After finishing up at GA, I took a role as a software developer atRegeneron Pharmacueticals. For almost 5 years, I created and maintained a wide range of applications for pharamecutical research. I also learned a lot about cutting edge science. Since July, 2019, I've been a Lead Instructor of Software Engineering atFlatiron School. There, I teach burgeoning software developers how to create full stack web applications.
